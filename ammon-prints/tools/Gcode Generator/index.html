What's GCODE?

GCODE most commonly refers to the instructions given to a CNC-machine, a 3D-Printer in our case, to create, or print, a model. Most modern FDM-printers use this standard, although some instructions may vary between each firmware: in Marlin-flavoured GCODE, the instruction "G10" refers to a retraction, where as in Duet-flavoured GCODE, "G10" refers to the temperature of the toolhead. There is of course some similarities between the versions: "G28" refers to the "homing" process of the 3D-Printer, where the different axis of the printer will try to find the endstop and determine the position accordingly. Some instructions can be used with parameter, such as "G28X" will only home the X-axis of the printer. For more informations, you can always refer to the Wikipedia page, the Duet's dictionnary, or the RepRap Wiki.
